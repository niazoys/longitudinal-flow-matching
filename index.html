Abstract

Generative models for sequential data often struggle with sparsely sampled and high-dimensional trajectories, typically reducing the learning problem to pairwise transitions. We propose Interpolative Multi-Marginal Flow Matching (IMMFM), which learns continuous stochastic dynamics jointly consistent with multiple observed time points. IMMFM uses a piecewise-quadratic interpolation path as a smooth target for flow matching and jointly optimizes drift with a data-driven diffusion coefficient, supported by a theoretical condition for stable learning. Across synthetic and real longitudinal neuroimaging benchmarks, IMMFM improves forecasting quality and downstream clinical utility.

Figure 1. IMMFM overview: sparse observations, uncertainty-aware stochastic dynamics, and trajectory forecasting pipeline.

Methodology

  • Multi-marginal objective: IMMFM learns trajectories jointly across multiple time points instead of chaining independent pairwise transports.
  • Piecewise-quadratic path: A smooth conditional Gaussian path reduces velocity discontinuities from simple linear interpolation.
  • Drift + score + diffusion: The model jointly learns probability-flow drift, score, and a learned diffusion coefficient tied to predictive uncertainty.
  • Theory: The paper proves a gradient-equivalence condition showing uncertainty learning does not bias the core drift/score optimization at stationary points.
Figure 2. On S-shaped and σ-shaped synthetic trajectories, IMMFM better tracks curved and crossing dynamics than prior flow-matching baselines.

Main Results

On ADNI, Brain MS, and Brain GBM, IMMFM variants outperform competitive Neural ODE/SDE, diffusion, and flow baselines. Reported gains include +1.0 to +4.4% Dice, +1.5 to +2.2 dB PSNR, and +1.2 to +4.5% SSIM. For highly stochastic cohorts, the uncertainty-aware SU-IMMFM variant is strongest.

The disease-progression analysis also shows downstream value: in ADNI, AD-vs-CN classification improves from 71.7% at ~18 months to 80.8% using model-forecasted ~36 month trajectories.

Appendix Highlights: Ablations and Extra Experiments

The appendix confirms that all three proposed components contribute meaningfully. The piecewise-quadratic path yields the largest gains (up to +3.7% Dice, >2.0 dB PSNR), learned diffusion improves difficult stochastic data (e.g., GBM HD reduction >6.2), and previous-frame conditioning improves trajectory consistency (up to +2.1% Dice).

Temporal generalization and 3D extension

Appendix analyses show expected degradation with increasing forecasting horizon, with the steepest drop in the most unpredictable GBM trajectories. Additional 3D experiments show stronger volumetric forecasting versus 2D, including ADNI Dice improvements from 0.920 to 0.947 and major Hausdorff-distance reductions.

These analyses support the main paper narrative: IMMFM improves short-horizon fidelity while remaining useful over longer horizons, and the gains transfer from 2D slices to full 3D volumetric disease progression modeling.
